Our Mission

We noticed that at any location, ranging from our schools, public parks, our parentsâ€™ companies, and even Amazon the trash cans for various functions are treated the same. With the same items inside although they have very different functions... Our goal is to educate people on the consequences of sorting items incorrectly while providing them clear guidelines on where to place items. We hope this website will prevent you from ever having to stand awkwardly in front of the trash cans again and having to impulsively make a random decision. We want others to understand where to put what items without having to break a sweat, in addition, understand the power and impact on the environment you have just from where you decide to throw your post-lunch trash!.

Compost

What is it?

    Biodegradable: material capable of being broken down by bacteria or other living organisms

  • Items that ARE compostable:

    • Fruit and Vegetable Scraps
    • All Plant-Based Milk (almond, soy, cashew, etc.)
    • Bread, Tortilla, Pitas
    • Cooked Grains
    • Coffee Grounds/Loose Leaf Tea
    • Egg shells

  • Items that ARE NOT compostable:

    • Meats, bones, fish, dairy

  • Golden Rule: compost is almost anything that has been alive and can be broken down by bacteria or other living things (excluding cooked food)

    Recycle

    What is it?

      Turning waste into more useful and reusable material

    • Items that ARE recyclable:

      • Plastic (bottles, containers)
      • Cardboard
        • Cereal boxes, mail, magazines, newspaper
      • Metals
        • Cans
        • Glass
      • Food containers or jars
      • Soft drinks, beer bottles
      • Wine, liquior bottles

    • Items that ARE NOT recyclable:

      • Napkins and paper towels (used and unused)
      • Heat resistant or broken glass
      • Plastic grocery bags
      • Shredded, wet, or heavily dyed paper
      • Pizza boxes, styrofoam
      • Pottery and ceramics

    Trash

    What is it?

      Non-biodegradable and non-recyclable waste

    • Examples of trash:

      • Diapers
      • Menstrual products
      • Incandescent and halogen light bulbs
      • Mixed materials (ex: paper items with a plastic lining in them)
      • Disposable items (gloves, razors, etc.)
      • Broken glass
      • Animal feces

    • Where does trash go?

        • Just over 52 percent of the country's garbage ends up in one of more than 3,000 active landfills scattered throughout the U.S.
